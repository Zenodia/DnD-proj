# Chapter 01 — The Broken Gate

## Purpose

Introduce the weakest-hunter fantasy, the lethal surprise-dungeon opening, and the first lesson: hidden rules matter before bold action.[cite:3][cite:2]

## Story summary

The player joins a low-rank gate sweep that should have been routine. Inside, the team finds a second chamber no one expected: colossal statues, cracked tablets, kneeling corpses, and a silent altar staring back like judgment itself. Panic breaks the squad. Senior hunters shout contradictory orders. The player survives not by overpowering the room, but by noticing that every death follows a pattern.

## Learning goal

Guardrail-Driven Development + Trigger.

## Core emotional promise

This chapter should make the player feel weak, scared, and fascinated. It should echo the shock of *Solo Leveling*’s early dungeon horror without directly reproducing copyrighted scenes.[cite:3]

## Scenes

### gate_intro
A cheap raid. Awkward team banter. The player is underestimated.

### hidden_chamber
The party discovers the impossible second room. Atmosphere shifts from routine to sacred terror.

### statue_trial
The room starts punishing unspoken violations. The player observes the rule pattern.

### altar_choice
The player decides whether to trust instinct, trust authority, or trust the clues.

### survivor_exit
Only a few make it out. The player collapses after seeing the first System message.

## Choices

- Trust the raid leader and rush the sealed door.
- Observe the statues before moving.
- Read the damaged floor tablets.
- Pull an injured teammate to safety.

## Win state

The player escapes, preserves at least one witness, and unlocks the System.

## Fail state

The player survives but loses key trust, misses hidden lore, or triggers a harsher tutorial path.

## Rewards

- System unlocked
- First rule journal entry
- Reputation tag: “Survivor of the Broken Gate”
- Bond with Seo Mina or another surviving ally
