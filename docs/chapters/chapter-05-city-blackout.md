# Chapter 05 — City Blackout

## Purpose

Show that even overwhelming combat power fails if knowledge is not stored and warnings are not shared in time.

## Story summary

A chain of gate events hits the city in rapid succession. Teams clear fights but still lose ground because reports do not connect, old patterns are forgotten, and civilians receive warnings too late. The player realizes the city itself has become the battlefield, and memory plus communication matter as much as strength.

## Learning goal

Persist + Notify.

## Scenes

### first_breach
Two gates open within minutes of each other.

### lost_report
A prior anomaly file could have prevented disaster but was never surfaced.

### archive_hall
The Archivist introduces the idea that memory is a weapon.

### panic_wave
Civilians flood transit lines as warnings arrive late.

### relay_defense
The player uses the Shadow Guild to both fight and communicate.

## Choices

- Prioritize monster kills over reporting.
- Save data before chasing loot.
- Warn civilians early and lose tactical surprise.
- Hide information to preserve personal advantage.

## Win state

The player stabilizes the city by combining combat, memory, and communication.

## Fail state

The city survives, but trust in hunters collapses, increasing difficulty in the final act.

## Rewards

- Pattern archive unlocked
- World-state broadcast system
- Civilian trust meter
