# Game Overview

## Working title

**Solo Leveling: Agent Quest**

## Core pitch

A teen hunter survives a catastrophic gate, awakens a mysterious System, and grows from the weakest trainee into the leader of a tactical shadow-guild. The twist is that every victory teaches a real agentic design discipline: guardrails first, then specs, then tests, then orchestration, then fast experimentation.[cite:3]

## Audience

The primary player is someone who already enjoys *Solo Leveling* and will recognize the pleasure of rank progression, dungeon escalation, impossible boss fights, and the fantasy of turning weakness into mastery.[cite:3]

## Adaptation goal

This game should feel authentic to the original story’s momentum without trying to retell the original plot scene for scene. It should echo the original structure: weak hunter, deadly tutorial, secret growth mechanism, increasingly dangerous gates, summoned allies, and the burden of protecting others.[cite:3]

## Design pillars

- Power fantasy with earned progression.
- Dark gates, cinematic danger, and sudden reversals.
- System messages that feel mysterious, not overly tutorialized.
- Strategic mastery through the 7-step pattern.
- Educational mechanics hidden inside a fun hunter fantasy.

## Non-goals

- Do not directly recreate Sung Jin-woo’s exact story events, dialogue, or named plot reveals.
- Do not turn the game into a classroom worksheet with monsters pasted on top.
- Do not explain every concept before play; teach through quests, consequences, and upgrades.
